# Privacy Policy — Happy BirThey

**Effective date:** 8 June 2026

Happy BirThey ("the app", "we", "us") is a birthday reminder app built around a
simple promise: **your data stays in your iCloud, and we can't even see it.**

This policy explains, in plain language, what the app does and does not do with your
information.

---

## The short version

- We do **not** collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any of your personal data.
- We do **not** have any servers, accounts, logins, or databases that hold your data.
- We do **not** use analytics, advertising, tracking, or third-party SDKs.
- The birthdays, names, photos, and notes you add live **only** on your device and in
  **your own private iCloud account** — which is controlled by Apple and accessible only
  to you. The developer has no way to read it.

---

## What information the app handles

All of the following is created and used **on your device** (and synced through your own
private iCloud account). None of it is ever sent to the developer.

| Information | Why the app uses it | Where it is stored |
|---|---|---|
| Names, birth dates, photos, notes of the people you add | To show birthdays and send you reminders | On your device + your private iCloud (CloudKit) |
| Your own name and birthday | To celebrate your birthday and address you by name | On your device (local settings only) |
| Your language preference | To display the app in your chosen language | On your device (local settings only) |
| Notification permission | To deliver local birthday reminders | Managed by iOS |

We, the developer, **never receive a copy** of any of this.

## iCloud sync (CloudKit)

The app uses Apple's **CloudKit** to keep your birthdays in sync across your own devices.
Your records are stored in your **private CloudKit database**, which is part of your
personal iCloud account. By design, **the developer cannot access, view, or retrieve
data in your private CloudKit database** — only you can, through your own Apple ID.

iCloud is provided by Apple and governed by
[Apple's Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/).

## Photos

When you choose a photo for a person, it is selected through Apple's standard photo
picker, which does not grant the app access to your photo library. The single photo you
pick is stored with that person's record on your device and in your private iCloud — and
nowhere else.

## Notifications

Birthday reminders are **local notifications** scheduled on your device. They are not
sent from any server. The app does not send marketing or push messages.

## What we collect: nothing

The app contains **no analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising, no trackers, and no
third-party code**. We do not build a profile of you, and there is no "data collection"
to opt out of, because there is none.

## Children's privacy

Because the app collects no data at all, it collects no data from children. Anything a
child enters stays on their device and in their family's iCloud, like any other personal
note.

## Keeping and deleting your data

You are always in control:

- **Delete a single entry** by removing that person in the app.
- **Delete everything** by deleting the app, which removes its on-device data. To also
  remove the synced copy, sign in to iCloud settings and clear the app's iCloud data, or
  delete the app's data from any device signed in to the same Apple ID.

Since we hold none of your data, there is nothing for us to delete on our side.

## Your rights

Privacy laws such as the GDPR (EU) and KVKK (Türkiye) give you rights over your personal
data. Because all your data remains under your own control in your device and iCloud, and
we never receive it, you can exercise those rights directly — by editing or deleting your
data in the app at any time, without needing to contact us.
